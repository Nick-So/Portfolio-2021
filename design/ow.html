Overview

Problem

Overwatch is over 5 years old, and its age is beginning to show - especially when you compare it to other competitive multiplayer games.

  • Menus navigation feels clunky with too much friction
  • The process of sharing gameplay highlights is too cumbersome
  • Coordinating team aesthetics with other players is difficult

Goal

Update the game's menus to give players more agency and keep the game feeling fresh.

  • Make menus more intuitive and informative
  • Let players easily share their experience to drive social engagement
  • Encourage team play by allowing groups to quickly coordinate their look

User Research

Research Plan

Interviews

  • Casual Overwatch Players
  • Competitive Overwatch Players
  • Gamers who enjoy online multiplayer games

Survey for Quantitative Data

Insights

  • Most users are casual players, but they also care about performing well.
  • Most casual players enjoy the game with friends and want a way to coordinate their team aesthetic.
  • Hardcore competitive players generally care less about character aesthetics.
  • Players want an easier/quicker way to share gameplay highlights.
  • The "Hero Gallery" menu is the most in need of an update.

Personas

We created two User Personas to represent the two most common user types based on our research.

Mandy is a young woman who plays casually with her sister, and cares mostly about having fun and making her characters look good.


Xavier is a college student who plays competitively. He cares most about his gameplay stats and showing off his skills.

Prototyping and Iteration

Wireframing

After determining which features were the highest priority, we began wireframing the UI to get an idea of how those features might appear in the game.

Our first pass pulled ideas from popular PC games, like League of Legends. However, we quickly realized that such designs would not work for Overwatch which is available on consoles as well.


We also tried to recreate and update existing UI features, but decided not to re-invent the wheel. Instead we simply added buttons to screenshots of the game if we knew that the UI elements would largely remain the same.


However, some of the menus in our design did need to be created from scratch, so we did our best to recreate the look and feel of the game's UI.

Hi-Fidelity Prototype

Once we reached a consensus on how to proceed, we performed some user tests to make sure our designs were clear and intuitive - even for new users who were unfamiliar with Overwatch.

The largest design overhaul appears in the "Hero Gallery". In the screenshot above you can see that we expanded user agency by adding the following features:

  • "New!" section to show most recent unlocks
  • Loadout Presets to allow users to quickly swap their gear
  • Item Previews to show users what an item will look like before it's equipped
  • Item Favorites so players can mark their favorite items and find them quickly.
  • Background Select to allow players to change the background image, so they can coordinate their look based on the map.

The Final Prototype

Conclusion

Reflection

Overwatch's menus are clean and minimal, but perhaps too much so. They introduce moments of friction and lack information in service of their appearance. Modern gamers are accustomed to having more control and information at their fingertips, and the lack of that agency makes the game feel a bit dated and out-of-touch. By refreshing the menu UX with quality-of-life improvements, we can quickly, easily, and cheaply breathe fresh life into the game.

Direction

Next Steps:

  • More testing!
    • Refine information presented based on user feedback and desires
    • Test our designs on a console input (controller) to ensure compatibility.
  • Give players even more control and expand the flow for sharing Gameplay Highlights
